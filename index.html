About

A voice carried from
the mountains to the score

Yosif Namiq is a composer and pianist from Kurdistan whose work moves between intimacy and grandeur. Rooted in the melodic language of his homeland and shaped by the emotional grammar of film scoring, his music turns memory and landscape into sound.

Every piece begins the same way — alone, at the piano, with a single phrase allowed to grow until it is felt before it is understood. From that first phrase, orchestras rise, strings swell, and the cinematic takes shape.

"I don't write music to be heard. I write it to be remembered."
